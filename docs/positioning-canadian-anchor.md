# Propharmex Positioning Lexicon — Canadian-Anchored

**What this document is.** The single source of truth for how Propharmex describes itself on every public surface. Whenever a content writer or a Claude session reaches for a hero, lede, eyebrow, capability subhead, or any other positioning string, they copy from this doc rather than improvise. Authority sits with this lexicon, [`CLAUDE.md`](../CLAUDE.md) §1, and [`docs/brand-voice.md`](./brand-voice.md), in that order. Drift is treated as a defect.

**Why this exists.** Propharmex was previously described in "two-hub" language (e.g., "operating hubs in Mississauga and Hyderabad"). That framing positioned the company as "binational" and risked under-leading the Canadian regulatory anchor. The new positioning leads Canadian; the Indian development centre in Hyderabad is named honestly only where it materially belongs (`/facilities`, `/facilities/hyderabad-india`, and `/quality`).

---

## Section 1 — The flagship sentence

Use as the one-breath introduction in heroes, OG descriptions, social cards, sales decks, and any other surface that has a single sentence to introduce the company.

> **Propharmex is a Canadian pharmaceutical services company anchored at our Mississauga DEL site.**

Thirteen words. The regulatory anchor is in the sentence. Do not extend it with a "with hubs in…" clause.

## Section 2 — Lead identity copy (three lengths)

Three approved variants. Pick the variant that fits the surface; do not paraphrase mid-doc.

### 25-word version — footer microcopy, OG description

> Canadian pharmaceutical services company anchored at our Mississauga, Ontario site under Health Canada Drug Establishment Licence. Pharmaceutical development, analytical services, regulatory affairs, and 3PL distribution for drug developers globally.

### 60-word version — homepage hero lede, long OG, social-share descriptions

> Propharmex is a Canadian pharmaceutical services company. Our operations are anchored at our Mississauga, Ontario site under our Health Canada Drug Establishment Licence, with 3PL distribution running from the same facility. We support drug developers worldwide — US generic sponsors, Canadian innovators, and multilateral procurement agencies — across pharmaceutical development, analytical services, regulatory affairs, and distribution. Programmes file in Canada and reach markets globally.

### 120-word version — /about lede, /why-propharmex lede, capability-deck cover page

> Propharmex is a Canadian pharmaceutical services company. Our operations are anchored at our Mississauga, Ontario site, where we hold our Health Canada Drug Establishment Licence and run our 3PL distribution footprint for the Canadian market. The regulatory anchor and the client-of-record relationship sit in Canada. Behind the Canadian-facing operations, an offshore development centre under our quality system provides operational depth — formulation, method development, analytical services, and stability work that compounds the value our Canadian clients receive. Programmes file in Canada and reach markets worldwide. Our clients include US generic sponsors filing ANDAs, Canadian innovators preparing NOC submissions, and multilateral procurement agencies sourcing under WHO prequalification. Capabilities span end-to-end pharmaceutical development, analytical services, regulatory affairs, and distribution.

*Note:* /about and /why-propharmex are not exemption surfaces. The 120-word variant therefore refers to "an offshore development centre" rather than naming Hyderabad. If a writer needs to name the development centre on these pages, they need an explicit positioning carve-out — escalate.

## Section 3 — The Hyderabad framing sentence

A single approved sentence (≤30 words) that names Hyderabad as operational depth. Use this sentence verbatim wherever a non-exemption surface has a legitimate technical reason to nod at the development centre — for example, a service capability page that needs to acknowledge formulation depth without breaking out the site map.

> **Our Indian development centre in Hyderabad provides the formulation, method-development, and stability depth that supports our Canadian programmes.**

Nineteen words. Names the discipline depth. Frames Hyderabad as supporting, not co-equal. If a surface does not have that legitimate technical reason, do not name Hyderabad at all.

## Section 4 — The QMS-scope sentence (regulatory exemption surface)

Use only on `/quality` and `/quality-compliance`. These are the two surfaces where the QMS scope must be honestly described to procurement reviewers and audit clients. The paragraph names Hyderabad explicitly — the regulatory record requires it.

> Our quality management system is anchored at our Mississauga site under the Health Canada Drug Establishment Licence and the Canadian *Food and Drug Regulations* Part C, Division 1A. Our Indian development centre in Hyderabad operates under the same QMS — unified procedures, harmonized SOPs, audit-trailed change control, and a single document stream — and contributes formulation, method-development, analytical, and stability work to programmes that file in Canada. The DEL anchor is Mississauga; the QMS scope honestly extends to Hyderabad.

Seventy-eight words. Three regulatory anchors named (DEL, *Food and Drug Regulations* Part C/Division 1A, the QMS itself). One Hyderabad mention, framed as "operates under the same QMS". Do not use this paragraph elsewhere.

## Section 5 — Service execution copy

Service capability matrices and lifecycle steppers stop labelling sites. The work is real; the breakdown is internal. The replacement language reads as "Propharmex executes" without naming Mississauga or Hyderabad inside a matrix row.

### Replacement subhead phrases (pick one per page; do not mix)

- **Executed by Propharmex** — neutral, default
- **Run by the Propharmex team** — slightly warmer; for capability summaries
- **Within the Propharmex programme** — for procurement-flavoured pages where "programme" already appears

### Worked matrix-row rewrites

| OLD | NEW |
|---|---|
| Method development authored at Hyderabad; release testing executed at Mississauga | Method development and release testing — executed by Propharmex |
| Stability studies run at our Hyderabad ICH zone IVb chambers | Stability studies — including ICH Q1A(R2) zone IVb conditions for hot-and-humid markets |
| Formulation work happens at Hyderabad; tech transfer is owned at Mississauga | Formulation development and tech transfer — single team, single QMS |
| Analytical services span both hubs (HPLC + LC-MS/MS in Hyderabad; release-bench HPLC in Mississauga) | Analytical services — full panel: HPLC, LC-MS/MS, dissolution, stability monitoring, impurity profiling |
| Regulatory affairs authored at Hyderabad and filed from Mississauga | Regulatory affairs — authored by Propharmex, filed under our Health Canada Drug Establishment Licence |
| executed at Mississauga (primary) and supported at Hyderabad (secondary) | executed by Propharmex |

When a procurement reviewer asks "where is each capability run?", the answer is the site briefing pack under NDA — which is the existing facilities posture (`docs/content-style-guide.md` anonymization rules). Do not re-publish that map on service pages.

## Section 6 — Banned phrases → preferred phrases

The `brand-voice-guardian` skill auto-flags any of the banned phrases below on a public-surface page. Use this table when revising existing copy or drafting new.

| BANNED | PREFERRED | NOTE |
|---|---|---|
| "two hubs", "two operating hubs", "two-hub model" | "anchored at Mississauga", "Canadian-anchored", "Canadian pharmaceutical services company" | The geography exists, but "two-hub" framed Propharmex as a binational company. New posture: Canadian, with Indian operational depth. |
| "Canada-India", "Canada–India", "Canada-India playbook", "Canada-India bridge" | "Canadian", "from Canada", "filed in Canada" | "Canada-India" implied an intermediary offering. Propharmex is a Canadian company. |
| "Indian hub", "Indian site", "Hyderabad hub" | (omit on most surfaces) "Indian development centre", "operational depth" | On surfaces other than `/facilities`, `/facilities/hyderabad-india`, and `/quality`, do not name Hyderabad. Use "operational depth" or omit. |
| "operating from Mississauga and Hyderabad", "Mississauga + Hyderabad" | "operating from Mississauga, Ontario", "anchored at Mississauga" | The "+ Hyderabad" notation is the surface signature of two-hub framing. Drop it. |
| "co-equal hubs", "twin hubs", "sister sites" | "Canadian operations supported by our Indian development centre" | Hyderabad is not co-equal — it supports Canadian programmes. Framing matters for procurement honesty. |
| "bridge", "bridge service", "Canada-India bridge" | (omit; describe the actual service) | Propharmex does not offer a bridge service. Describe what we do, not the geographic flow. |
| "our two countries", "across our two geographies", "binational" | "our team", "Propharmex" | The corporate body is Canadian. Geographic plurals reintroduce two-hub framing. |
| "Hyderabad facility" (on a non-exemption surface) | (omit, or rewrite to "our development bench") | If Hyderabad needs to be named, the surface should be one of the three exemption pages. |
| "Canadian-Indian operating model", "the Indo-Canadian advantage" | "Canadian operating model with Indian development depth", "Canadian regulatory anchor with offshore development depth" | Word order matters: Canadian first, Indian-as-supporting. |
| "Propharmex Canada", "Propharmex India" (as sibling-brand naming) | "Propharmex · Mississauga, Ontario", "Propharmex · Hyderabad, Telangana" | Single corporate body. Site qualifiers replace sibling-brand naming. |

If you find an old phrase that is not in this table, follow the same logic: lead Canadian, name Hyderabad only on the exemption surfaces, drop "two-hub" notation, do not promote Hyderabad to co-equal.

## Section 7 — Sample sentence rewrites

Worked OLD → NEW examples for the surfaces writers and Claude sessions touch most often. Apply the pattern, not the exact words; the *posture* is what carries.

### 1. Homepage hero headline

- **OLD:** "Two hubs. One quality system. One record of work."
- **NEW:** "Drug development that files from Canada and reaches the world."
- *Note:* Drops "two hubs" entirely. Headline now centres on the value (Canadian filing, global reach) rather than the operating geography.

### 2. Homepage hero lede

- **OLD:** "Propharmex is a Canada-anchored pharmaceutical services company with operating hubs in Mississauga, Ontario and Hyderabad, Telangana, serving drug developers globally."
- **NEW:** "Propharmex is a Canadian pharmaceutical services company. Our operations are anchored at our Mississauga, Ontario site under Health Canada Drug Establishment Licence, supporting drug developers worldwide across development, analytical, regulatory, and distribution."
- *Note:* Identical structure (identity → anchor → service breadth) but lead is unambiguously Canadian. Hyderabad omitted from the lede.

### 3. /about page lede

- **OLD:** "Propharmex was founded as a two-hub CDMO: Mississauga gave us the regulatory anchor; Hyderabad gave us the analytical bench."
- **NEW:** "Propharmex was founded as a Canadian pharmaceutical services company under Health Canada Drug Establishment Licence. The Mississauga regulatory site has always been our home. Over time we deepened our formulation, method-development, and analytical work through an offshore development centre operating under our quality system — supporting Canadian programmes from the bench up."
- *Note:* Reframes the founding story Canadian-first. The offshore development centre enters as built-out depth, not as a co-founding hub. /about is **not** an exemption surface — it does not name Hyderabad. Writers who want to name the site here should use the /facilities link instead and let the user navigate to a surface where Hyderabad belongs.

### 4. /why-propharmex hero

- **OLD:** "We're the Canada-India CDMO that turns regulatory ambition into filed dossiers."
- **NEW:** "A Canadian DEL holder with the development depth to file under it."
- *Note:* Twelve-word headline. Canadian-anchored framing. "Development depth" gestures at the operational support without naming Hyderabad.

### 5. Service capability matrix subhead

- **OLD:** "What runs at Mississauga, what runs at Hyderabad — under one quality system"
- **NEW:** "What we run, end to end — under one quality system"
- *Note:* Drops site-of-execution disclosure on the service page. Procurement reviewers can request the site map under NDA — that's the existing facilities posture.

### 6. /facilities hub-card eyebrow (Mississauga + Hyderabad — both shown for contrast)

- **OLD (Mississauga):** "Propharmex Canada · Mississauga, Ontario"
- **NEW (Mississauga):** "Propharmex · Mississauga, Ontario · Head office and DEL site"
- **OLD (Hyderabad):** "Propharmex India · Hyderabad, Telangana"
- **NEW (Hyderabad):** "Propharmex · Hyderabad, Telangana · Indian development centre"
- *Note:* Both eyebrows drop the "Propharmex Canada / Propharmex India" sibling-brand naming. Mississauga is positioned as head office; Hyderabad is positioned as the development centre under the same parent. One company, two sites with explicit roles.

### 7. /quality scope statement

- **OLD:** "Our quality system spans both hubs — Mississauga and Hyderabad — under one DEL."
- **NEW:** "Our quality management system is anchored at our Mississauga site under the Health Canada Drug Establishment Licence. Our Indian development centre in Hyderabad operates under the same QMS — unified procedures, harmonized SOPs, audit-trailed change control."
- *Note:* See Section 4 for the longer regulatory-honesty paragraph. This shorter form is appropriate for a section subhead under a `/quality` header. Anchors DEL clearly to Mississauga; does not promote Hyderabad to co-equal.

### 8. Case-study problem statement (anonymized client)

- **OLD:** "A Top-5 US generic manufacturer came to Propharmex with a failed first ANDA. We ran the analytical rebuild out of Hyderabad and refiled from Mississauga."
- **NEW:** "A Top-5 US generic manufacturer came to Propharmex with a failed first ANDA. We rebuilt the dissolution method, revalidated per ICH Q2(R2), and refiled the dossier under our Health Canada Drug Establishment Licence."
- *Note:* Drops site-of-execution from the case-study narrative. Technical work (method rebuild, ICH Q2(R2)) speaks for itself. Refile happens "under DEL" — Mississauga implied without naming. Anonymization rule from `docs/content-style-guide.md` §Anonymization preserved.

### 9. Insight article meta-description (≤160 chars)

- **OLD:** "How Canada–India CDMOs run a unified QMS across two hubs. Inside our two-hub operating model." (97 chars)
- **NEW:** "How a Canadian CDMO files dossiers in Canada and runs a unified quality system through to global development depth." (115 chars)
- *Note:* Lead is Canadian. "Global development depth" gestures at offshore capability without naming a country. Within SERP truncation budget.

## Application notes

- **Three exemption surfaces.** Hyderabad may be named on `/facilities`, `/facilities/hyderabad-india`, and `/quality` (and `/quality-compliance` where it exists). On the `/about` founding-story arc, naming once is permitted; outside that arc, omit. Everywhere else, omit.
- **Regulatory honesty trumps tone.** If a regulatory claim (WHO-GMP, CDSCO licensing, the Canadian QMS reach into Hyderabad) materially affects what a reviewer needs to know, name Hyderabad and link the primary source. Do not soften a regulatory claim to fit the new tone.
- **Site qualifiers, not sibling brands.** Use "Propharmex · Mississauga, Ontario" and "Propharmex · Hyderabad, Telangana" instead of "Propharmex Canada" and "Propharmex India". Single corporate body.
- **Capability matrices stop disclosing site of execution.** The site-execution map lives in the briefing pack under NDA, per the existing `/facilities` posture. Do not re-publish it on service pages.
- **`brand-voice-guardian` is the gate.** Every commit that revises positioning copy hands off to the gate before merge. Section 6's banned-phrase table is the canonical detection list.
