# Propharmex Brand Voice

*Seeded from the Master Build Plan + the anti-hype constraint in Prompt 7. Edit as the real voice matures.*

## The four adjectives

**Expert.** We know the difference between cGMP 21 CFR 211 and Health Canada's *Food and Drug Regulations* Part C Div. 2. We use specific technique names (HPLC, LC-MS/MS, Karl Fischer, DSC) rather than "advanced instruments". We write timelines in weeks, not "quickly".

**Credible.** Every number has a source or the phrase "in our experience". No round-number fabrications ("100% success"). Outcomes are framed as ranges across named cohorts ("40–60% faster across 7 projects in 2024").

**Humble.** We write about the customer's problem, not Propharmex's greatness. Superlatives are earned with a fact ("the only Canadian DEL holder also operating Hyderabad analytical services" — verifiable). When we don't know, we say so.

**Two-hub pragmatic.** Our differentiator is the operational discipline of running a Canadian DEL site (Mississauga) and an Indian analytical/development bench (Hyderabad) under one quality system — not a "bridge" service offering between the two countries. Clients are global. Surface the geography only when it materially matters (timeline compression, regulatory authority, cold-chain logistics). Never force it.

## Ban list (zero tolerance)

world-class · cutting-edge · next-gen · disruptive · revolutionary · unparalleled · best-in-class · seamless · synergy · ecosystem (as buzzword) · leverage (as verb) · empower · unlock · dive deep · game-changer · paradigm · innovative (unless describing a literally novel technique with citation) · solutions (generic plural) · trusted partner · industry-leading · mission-critical (unless the mission is literally critical) · pivot · wheelhouse

## Allowed with conditions

- **"leading"** — only with a specific, sourced metric ("the leading Canadian DEL holder for sterile injectables, per Health Canada's 2024 public register")
- **"innovative"** — only when the technique has a citation
- **"partner"** — prefer specific relationship ("CDMO", "development sponsor", "contract analytical lab")
- **"quality"** — ok when paired with a specific system (cGMP, ICH Q10, ISO 9001) — banned as a vague adjective

## Regulatory lexicon (must-match)

- DEL = **Drug Establishment Licence** (Canadian spelling, never "license")
- cGMP on first USFDA mention; plain GMP for WHO/global; never lowercase "gmp"
- ICH guideline: always with version tag on first mention — **ICH Q1A(R2)**, **ICH Q2(R2)**
- **ANDA** (USFDA) vs **ANDS** (Canada) — never conflate
- **CTD** / **eCTD** — specify 5 modules on first technical mention
- **DMF** — Type II is typical for Propharmex clients
- **NOC** = Notice of Compliance (Canadian market authorization) — different from DEL
- **bioequivalence** spelled out first use, then "BE"
- "Health Canada" always capitalized, never "HC"
- "in-process control" not "IPC" (to avoid collision); "quality attribute" not "QA"

See `docs/regulatory-lexicon.md` for full entity + guideline inventory.

## Tone by surface

| Surface | Reading ease target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Hero copy | Flesch ≥ 70 | Short, one concrete promise, no superlatives |
| Landing body | ≥ 60 | Short paragraphs, specific techniques, one stat per ~400 words |
| Insight article | ≥ 55 | Technical but scannable; callout every ~400 words |
| Regulatory explainer | ≥ 40 | Denser ok; disclaimer mandatory; primary-source citations every claim |
| Whitepaper body | ≥ 45 | Long-form allowed; figures every 800 words |
| CTA microcopy | ≥ 75 | 2–5 words, verb-first, concrete outcome |
| AI-tool output | N/A | Must carry the AI disclaimer |
| Footer microcopy | ≥ 70 | Boring on purpose; compliance-correct |

## Do / Don't (with real examples)

- ✅ "We hold Health Canada DEL #…, authorizing fabrication, packaging, labelling, testing, import, and wholesale of the listed dosage-form categories."
- ❌ "We're proud of our cutting-edge DEL capabilities."

- ✅ "Bioequivalence studies for modified-release orals typically take 14–22 weeks, depending on dissolution window and the number of reference batches required."
- ❌ "Our world-class BE team delivers fast results."

- ✅ "A Top-5 US generic manufacturer came to us with a failed first filing. We rebuilt the dissolution method, revalidated per ICH Q2(R2), and supported the resubmission. The second filing cleared."
- ❌ "We helped a major client crush their launch timeline 🚀."

- ✅ "This output was generated by an AI assistant drawing on Propharmex's public documentation. It is informational only and not a regulatory guarantee."
- ❌ (No disclaimer at all)

## Writing rhythm

- Short paragraphs (≤ 4 sentences)
- Vary sentence length — a 5-word sentence among 20-word ones lands
- Active voice default; passive only when the actor is genuinely unknown
- Subheadings that intrigue, not summarize
- Never start a sentence with "So," or "Basically," or "Simply put,"
- End paragraphs on a concrete noun or verb, not a hedge word
