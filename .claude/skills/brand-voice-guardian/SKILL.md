# Brand Voice Guardian

The last line of defense before copy ships. Returns a pass, a conditional pass with edits applied, or a refuse with reasons.

## Voice contract (single source of truth: `docs/brand-voice.md`)

Expert · Credible · Humble · Zero-hype · Regulatory-precise · Canada-India pragmatic

## Detection rules

### Ban list (any occurrence → flag)

`world-class`, `cutting-edge`, `next-gen`, `disruptive`, `revolutionary`, `unparalleled`, `best-in-class`, `seamless`, `synergy`, `ecosystem` (when used as buzzword), `leverage` (as verb), `empower`, `unlock`, `dive deep`, `game-changer`, `paradigm`, `innovative` (unless literally describing a novel technique with citation), `solutions` (as a generic plural — ban unless specific), `trusted partner` (hollow).

### Superlative audit

Any "best", "leading", "top", "fastest", "largest", "only" → require:
(a) quantified comparison ("the only Canadian DEL holder also running Hyderabad analytical services" — specific), OR
(b) replace with humble framing ("one of a small number of…", "among our strengths is…")

### Regulatory terminology lock

- DEL = Drug Establishment **Licence** (not "license")
- cGMP on first USFDA mention; GMP plain for WHO/global; never just "gmp" lowercase
- "bioequivalence" spelled out first use, then "BE"
- "ICH Q1A(R2)" form — never "ICH Q1A" without the revision tag on first use
- "Health Canada" capitalized, no abbreviation (not "HC")
- "Canada-India bridge" — with the en-dash if the design system uses it in headings, plain hyphen in body

### Claim audit

For every factual, numeric, or regulatory claim:
- Is there a citation (inline link, footnote, or "in our experience") within 2 sentences?
- If about a timeline or approval, is there an "as of [date]" anchor?
- If about a competitor, is the comparison fair, sourced, and non-disparaging?
- If about a client, is the anonymization rule from `docs/content-style-guide.md` respected?

### Disclaimer audit (regulatory content)

Content that mentions DEL, ANDA, USFDA, ICH, WHO-GMP, TGA, EMA, or medical outcomes **must** carry the standard disclaimer block exactly once. Missing → auto-insert and flag.

### AI-output audit

Content that came from one of the 4 AI features **must** carry:
> *This output was generated by an AI assistant drawing on Propharmex's public documentation. It is informational only and not a regulatory guarantee. Contact our team for a review tailored to your specific filing.*

Missing → refuse to ship.

### Passive-voice audit

Flag any paragraph where > 40% of sentences are passive. Suggest active rewrites, preserving meaning. Exception: regulatory prose where the actor is genuinely unknown (e.g., "the assay was validated per ICH Q2(R2)").

### Readability audit

Body prose target: Flesch Reading Ease ≥ 55 (roughly 10th-grade). Insight articles can be denser (≥ 45). Regulatory explainers can be lower (≥ 40). Hero and CTA copy should be ≥ 70.

```md
## Open issues (if REFUSE)
- The claim "faster than any US CDMO" is unquantified and potentially disparaging — needs source or removal
- The regulatory disclaimer is missing from this page

## Metrics
- Banned words: 0
- Unsourced regulatory claims: 0
- Passive rate: 22%

```

## Hand-offs

- If REFUSE with structural issues → route back to the originating writer skill
- If PASS_WITH_EDITS → applies edits in place (Sanity, MDX, or TSX strings)
- On PASS → emit a green ✓ for the calling prompt's DoD checklist
